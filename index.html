Anti Noise Whisper™ Trim

Valves controlling high pressure drop liquids and gases can contribute substantially to ambient noise levels. The noise can be generated in three basic ways: by the mechanical vibration of valve components, by turbulent gas flow (aerodynamic noise), or by cavitating liquid flow (hydrodynamic noise). If control valve noise is left untreated, it can introduce process control issues, pose safety risks to workers, or require costly repairs to valves, pipes, other instrumentation, and surrounding equipment.

Fisher™ Whisper™ solutions the best noise abatement reducing the noise at source.

Emerson’s Fisher™ Whisper™ technology, NXG are the new state-of-the-art products designed for noise abatement using additive manufacturing techniques. The innovative lattice design by our product engineering is realized into an actual product using additive manufacturing.

Additive manufacturing enables manufacturing of complex three-dimensional objects by adding material, layer by layer, from a digital model. However, beyond its innovative manufacturing capabilities, additive manufacturing is emerging as a key player in promoting sustainability. One of the primary ways in which 3D printing contributes to sustainability is through material efficiency. Unlike traditional subtractive manufacturing processes such as turning, milling, drilling etc., which often results in significant material wastage, additive manufacturing only uses the exact amount of material needed to build a product, minimizing waste and conserving resources.

Furthermore, the traditional noise abatement trims have limited capacity due to the limitations in manufacturing process. The Fisher™ Whisper™ NXG trim for globe control valves allows use of smaller valves significantly reducing cost and the carbon footprint, providing %20 more flow capacity than the traditional noise abatement solutions.

Engineered Deadband

PST in Antisurge Valves and what is the current issue:

A PST (Partial Stroke Test) assist in confirming an anti-surge valve’s functionality and availability. It can also provide continuous insight into the overall health of the valve assembly, which would give the plant more information to determine when and if maintenance is required.

PST performed with digital positioners often collects an array of different variables, which can be analyzed to determine common failure scenarios including sticking valve trim, loose air connections and improperly tightened valve packing, just to name a few.

Unfortunately, performing a PST on a normally closed anti-surge valve, or even just barely cracking such a valve off its seat, creates a substantial drop in discharge pressure and diverts compressor flow from the downstream process, causing significant process upsets. The plant in the above example needed a way to move the valve enough to prove it worked without passing enough flow to disrupt unit operations.

Engineered
Deadband Solution

Characterized Fisher Whisper Trim III with deadband from 0 to 5% valve opening

Only “Clearance Flow” allowed, 1% or less of rated Cv

No substantial flow passed - no effect on process control

The Solution:
Engineered flow deadband

The solution was a modification to the valve trim that allowed the valve to move enough to complete the PST and collect useful data while passing minimal flow during the test. This enabled the plant to perform PSTs as often as desired, each with minimal process impact.

When this engineered deadband is aligned with the required PST travel requirements, it allows the user to execute a PST on a normally closed valve and introduce a minimal — and acceptable — process disturbance.
